Our standing rules

  • Verified venues. A new venue is never published from a single, unconfirmed source — it needs corroboration from at least two independent sources (a festival programme, a second ticketing platform, an official venue site) before it appears on the map.
  • Stand-up only. Every listing passes a genre gate before publication — quizzes, movie nights, karaoke, and other non-stand-up events at comedy venues are filtered out, not shown alongside real shows.
  • The privacy relay is the product. COMEDY ATLAS shows public event and venue details only. No comedian's or organizer's personal contact info — email, phone — is ever displayed to another user. Every booking conversation goes through an anonymous relay; every ticket link points to the organizer's own official page.
  • No pay-to-rank. A show's position in search results is never influenced by whether its organizer pays us. Paid placement (when it exists) is always labeled as such.
  • Every fact is sourced. Each listing traces back to a specific source (an official venue site, a ticketing platform, a festival programme) — we don't invent or guess event details, and no AI model ever writes a canonical fact. A local model (never a paid API) may only draft a low-stakes suggestion for a human to approve or reject.
  • Ghost events get removed. When a show is cancelled or a listing turns out to be wrong, it comes down — see the counts above for how often that happens, not just that it can.

Where the data comes from

COMEDY ATLAS aggregates public listings from official venue websites, festival programmes (Edinburgh Fringe, PBH's Free Fringe, Laughing Horse, and others), and public ticketing platforms (Eventbrite and similar). We link directly to each organizer's own ticket page — we never resell tickets or take a cut of the door.
