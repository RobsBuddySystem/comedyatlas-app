Module 10: Submitting to Booked Shows
2026-07-21 · COMEDY ATLAS

This module is a structured outline -- a full write-up is coming soon.

This module is a structured stub — a full outline with the right books already attached, expanded to a full module soon. It covers the step beyond open mics: applying for a spot on a curated, booked lineup — what a booker actually wants to see, how to submit, and what happens after you're on a bill for the first time.

What this module will cover

  • The difference between an open-mic list and a booked, curated lineup
  • What a video submission needs to show a booker in under two minutes
  • Following a show's actual submission process instead of cold-messaging a promoter
  • What changes about your set once you're being paid or featured, even in a small way
  • Using COMEDY ATLAS's booker/venue profiles to see who's actually booking shows in your city, and how to apply

Sources to be drawn on

  • Stephen Rosenfield, *Mastering Stand-Up* (Chicago Review Press, 2017) — professional-track material selection
  • Logan Murray, *Get Started in Stand-Up Comedy* (Teach Yourself, 2015) — approaching promoters and securing a first booked spot
  • Ritch Shydner & Mark Schiff, *I Killed: True Stories of the Road from America's Top Comics* (Three Rivers Press, 2010) — the working comic's path from open mic to paid booking
