English stand-up comedy in Paris: the honest guide
2026-07-17 · COMEDY ATLAS

Paris has English-language stand-up, but it is not one single scene in one single room. It is a changing set of open mics, showcase nights, and visiting-comic style shows, mostly listed a few weeks at a time. This guide is for English-speaking locals, expats, and travellers who want to see stand-up in Paris without guessing from old blog posts or half-updated social pages.

The most useful thing to know is simple: check the live listings before you go. Lineups can change, rooms can shift, and the difference between an open mic and a showcase matters. An open mic is usually a lower-stakes night where comics test newer material. A showcase is generally built as a more polished show, often with a selected lineup. The current Comedy Atlas feed does not expose reliable price information for the Paris entries, so treat free-versus-paid status as something to confirm on the event page before you leave home.

If you want to see newer material and a mixed bill, start with an open mic listing such as The Open Mic Express. If you want the title to sound more like a curated night, look at Blast Off All Stars or Coucou Comedy's Friday Night Show. The event page is still the source of truth for the date you choose.

There are a few practical expectations to bring with you. First, times in the listings are start times: the current Paris feed includes 19:00, 19:30, and 20:00 starts. Second, venue names matter. Le Kibele and La Pomme d'Eve are both active in the current listings, but they host different nights at different times. Third, do not assume every English comedy listing is the same format. "Open mic," "All Stars," and "Friday Night Show" signal different kinds of evenings, even when they are all English stand-up.

Also, do not over-read the data. If an event page has no price in the Comedy Atlas feed, that does not mean it is free. If a listing says open mic, that does not guarantee the exact lineup. If a show is listed several times, that is useful evidence that the room is recurring, but the next specific date is still the thing to check.

For now, the honest version is this: English stand-up in Paris is real, active, and concentrated around a small number of recurring organizers in the current Comedy Atlas data.
